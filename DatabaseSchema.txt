KumariCinemas database notes

Main tables

CUSTOMER
- customer_id (PK)
- customer_name
- customer_contact
- username
- address

THEATRE
- theatre_id (PK)
- theatre_name

THEATRECITYHALL
- cityhall_id (PK)
- theatre_id (FK to THEATRE)
- cityhall_name
- cityhall_location

MOVIE
- movie_id (PK)
- movie_title
- duration
- movie_language
- movie_genre
- release_date

SHOW
- show_id (PK)
- cityhall_id (FK to THEATRECITYHALL)
- show_date
- show_time
- day_type

SHOW_PRICE
- pricing_id (PK)
- show_id (FK to SHOW)
- show_price
- pricing_reason

SEAT
- seat_id (PK)
- seat_no
- seat_status

BOOKING
- booking_id (PK)
- show_id (FK to SHOW)
- booking_status

PAYMENT
- payment_id (PK)
- payment_status
- payment_amount

TICKET
- ticket_id (PK)
- seat_id (FK to SEAT)
- ticket_price

Bridge tables

CUSTOMER_THEATRE
- customer_id
- theatre_id

CITYHALL_MOVIE
- cityhall_id
- movie_id

MOVIE_SHOW
- movie_id
- show_id

SHOW_SEAT
- show_id
- seat_id

CUSTOMER_BOOKING
- customer_id
- booking_id

BOOKING_PAYMENT
- booking_id
- payment_id

BOOKING_TICKET
- booking_id
- ticket_id

Important relationship notes

- One theatre can have many city halls
- One city hall belongs to one theatre
- One city hall can have many shows
- One show belongs to one city hall
- A movie and show are linked through MOVIE_SHOW
- A customer and booking are linked through CUSTOMER_BOOKING
- A booking and payment are linked through BOOKING_PAYMENT
- A booking and ticket are linked through BOOKING_TICKET
- A show and seat are linked through SHOW_SEAT

Oracle note

Some bridge table names in Oracle may have hyphens and quotes, like:
"Customer-Theatre"
"CityHall-Movie"
"Movie-Show"
"Show-Seat"
"Customer-Booking"
"Booking-Payment"
"Booking-Ticket"
